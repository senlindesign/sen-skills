# Medium — 只改壳,不改核

媒介适配改的是长度、排版、钩子、标签;不动的是观点和立场。壳入乡随俗,核是作者本人。

用户 style.md 的硬规则(Never 清单)与媒介惯例冲突时,style 优先。

先选语言,再选媒介:

- 中文稿默认只进入中文媒介:微信公众号、小红书、知乎、即刻、微博
- 英文稿默认只进入英文媒介:LinkedIn、Medium.com、Substack / Newsletter、X / Twitter、Threads、Reddit
- 用户要跨语言发布时,先按目标语言重写,再做媒介适配。不要把中文稿硬翻成英文媒介版,也不要把英文稿硬塞进中文媒介

拿不准某个媒介当下的流行形式 → WebSearch「{媒介} 内容风格 排版 趋势」,只看形式,不学内容。拿得准就用下面的静态规则,不搜。

**清单外的媒介**:用户点名的媒介不在下面 → 现场调研。搜该媒介的内容形式、长度惯例、钩子写法、排版与标签习惯,整理成与本文件同格式的一节,给用户确认后使用,并建议追加进本文件,下次就不用再搜。

## 中文媒介

### 微信公众号

**适合**:完整长文、观点文章、教程、复盘、系列文章。

**格式**:

- 标题 16-28 字,可以有态度,但不要标题党
- 正文 2000-5000 字;短观点也可以,但公众号优势在长文
- 开头 2-4 段直接进场景、冲突或核心判断,不写时代背景式空转
- 正文用 3-6 个小标题承重,每个小标题都带观点,不要只做内容标签
- 段落 2-4 句为主,保留段落留白;中英文之间加空格
- 可以放图、引用、延伸阅读或参考来源,但不靠配图补逻辑

**结尾**:

- 收在一句有余味的判断上,不做总结式复述
- CTA 用内容承诺,如「我会继续写 X」;不用「建议收藏」「前方干货」式情绪绑架

### 小红书

**适合**:经验拆解、教程、工具流、方法清单、反常识短观点、个人经历。

**格式**:

- 标题前 13 字定生死:给具体收益、具体痛点或认知张力
- 正文 600-1500 字;教程类可到 2000 字,也可转口播稿
- 开头 3 行内交代「我遇到什么问题 / 我发现什么 / 这篇给谁」
- 段落 1-2 句,手机上要能扫读
- emoji 可做小标题锚点,功能性使用,不要撒装饰
- 列表可以用,但每条必须有信息,不要把长文机械切碎
- tag 3-6 个:领域词 + 人群词 + 场景词

**结尾**:

- 用一个具体行动或一句判断收住
- 可以引导收藏/评论,但不要用夸张收益承诺

### 知乎

**适合**:解释型回答、观点辩护、争议问题、经验复盘、专业判断。

**格式**:

- 标题通常来自问题;如果是专栏文章,标题要像一个可辩论的判断
- 正文 1200-3500 字,允许比小红书和即刻更完整地铺逻辑
- 第一段先给短答案或核心立场,不要绕到第三屏才表态
- 先定义问题,再拆误区、机制、例子和边界
- 小标题用于推进论证,不要做「一、二、三」式公文结构
- 可以放反例、限定条件、数据或引用,但引用不能替你下判断

**结尾**:

- 收在问题的边界或一个更深的判断上
- 不做营销 CTA;可以邀请补充或讨论一个具体分歧点

### 即刻

**适合**:短观察、过程记录、失败复盘、做东西时的即时想法、长文先导观点。

**格式**:

- 150-500 字,单点直击
- 结构通常是:一个具体观察 + 一个判断 + 一个例子或后果
- 不用小标题,可以不配图;像跟同行说话
- 允许轻微口语和未完成感,但观点不能松
- 不解释所有背景,只给足让同温层接住的上下文

**结尾**:

- 收在最硬的一句判断上
- 不需要 CTA

### 微博

**适合**:热点反应、短判断、公共事件切入、文章金句分发。

**格式**:

- 140-800 字为主;长微博可以更长,但不要把公众号整篇搬过去
- 第一行直接给判断或事件切口
- 2-4 个短段落,每段只做一件事
- 适合用一个反问或一句锋利判断做转折,但不要连续反问
- hashtag 0-2 个,只用真实话题词,不堆领域词

**结尾**:

- 可以挂长文链接或评论区补充,但正文先独立成立
- 不用「转发扩散」式口吻

## English Media

### LinkedIn

**Best for**: professional reflection, builder notes, design/process lessons, career-adjacent arguments, launch narratives.

**Format**:

- 800-1300 characters for a compact post; 1300-2200 characters for a deeper post
- First two lines decide the click: start with a concrete moment, tension, or contrarian observation
- Paragraphs are 1-3 sentences; keep enough white space for mobile reading
- Use first person when there is real experience behind the point
- Give the why, not only the how
- Links go in comments when possible; hashtags 0-3 max

**Ending**:

- End on a clear judgment or practical consequence
- Avoid engagement bait such as "Agree?" or "Thoughts?"

### Medium.com

**Best for**: polished essays, evergreen arguments, technical reflections, long-form public writing.

**Format**:

- 1500-2500 words
- Title carries the claim; subtitle adds context or tension
- Opening uses a scene, personal moment, or direct claim; no generic industry setup
- Use attitude-driven section headings
- Put the core judgment in a pull quote if the piece needs one
- Images need captions; references belong at the end
- The piece should stand alone for readers who do not know the author

**Ending**:

- End with a resonant sentence, not a recap
- If there is a CTA, make it a content promise or newsletter subscription note

### Substack / Newsletter

**Best for**: direct audience relationship, essays, recurring columns, curation, behind-the-scenes notes.

**Format**:

- Ask which mode first: **Essay** or **Curation**
- Essay: 1000-2500 words, similar to Medium.com but more personal and serial
- Curation: 5-8 items; each item gets one observation + why it matters + link
- Opening can begin with a personal note, then bridge into the issue
- Use simple section breaks; avoid over-designed formatting
- Paid teaser, if needed, must list real deliverables only

**Ending**:

- Close with one sentence that leaves the reader somewhere sharper
- CTA can ask readers to subscribe, reply, or forward, but it should sound like the writer, not a growth template

### X / Twitter

**Best for**: sharp single ideas, threads, launch notes, public arguments, extracted lines from longer essays.

**Format**:

- Single post ≤280 characters
- Thread: 5-10 posts; first post is the hook, each post carries one idea
- Use plain language; no setup paragraph
- Put the strongest sentence first or last, not buried in the middle
- Use line breaks sparingly; no hashtag pile
- If linking, make the post valuable before the link

**Ending**:

- End a thread with a judgment, not a summary
- No generic "follow me for more"

### Threads

**Best for**: conversational short posts, softer public thoughts, community-facing observations, less compressed versions of X posts.

**Format**:

- Single post ≤500 characters
- Series: 3-6 posts, looser than X, more conversational
- Start with a relatable observation or direct claim
- Less performative than LinkedIn, less compressed than X
- Works well for one idea with a small story attached
- Hashtags usually unnecessary

**Ending**:

- End with a human sentence, not a slogan
- Questions are fine when they are real questions, not engagement bait

### Reddit

**Best for**: community discussion, problem framing, asking for critique, explaining a build/process to a specific subreddit.

**Format**:

- Always adapt to the target subreddit first; rules beat this guide
- Title should be a real question, concrete problem, or debatable claim
- 600-1500 words for substantive posts; shorter for quick questions
- Start with context: who you are, what you tried, what you are asking
- Use bullets only for clarity, not for marketing polish
- Disclose self-promotion, affiliation, or links clearly
- Avoid newsletter voice, launch copy, and founder-speak

**Ending**:

- Ask for one specific kind of feedback
- Do not end with a sales CTA
